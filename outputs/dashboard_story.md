# Dashboard Story

## Executive Summary

The executive dashboard shows a business with strong overall scale: 217,017,651.92 in sales, 33,306,312.84 in profit, and an overall profit margin of 15.35% across 4,200 orders. The strongest performance comes from the South region, Technology products, and the Home Office customer segment.

The main leadership message is that sales growth should be managed together with profit quality. The dashboard highlights clear opportunities in Technology, South, Home Office, and organic demand, but also shows risks from high discounts, Furniture returns, and slower Standard Class delivery.

## What Is Performing Well

South is the strongest region, with 64,693,706.73 in sales and 9,987,912.33 in profit. Technology is the strongest category, generating 153,895,304.22 in sales and 28,043,309.91 in profit. Within sub-categories, Copiers, Accessories, Phones, and Machines are major profit contributors.

Home Office is the strongest customer segment, with 74,500,746.01 in sales and 11,555,047.08 in profit. Organic is the largest campaign channel, generating 88,783,980.90 in sales and 13,444,456.85 in profit.

## What Is Underperforming

East has the lowest regional sales at 48,859,164.42. Furniture has weaker profit quality compared with Technology, producing 51,641,024.02 in sales but only 3,557,835.43 in profit.

Low-profit sub-categories such as Paper, Binders, Storage, Art, and Labels contribute much less profit than the leading Technology sub-categories. These areas may not be problems by themselves, but they should not receive the same investment priority as high-profit areas unless they support broader customer baskets.

## Visible Risks

Discount risk is visible in the dashboard. Orders with no discount generated 13,519,757.69 in profit, while orders with discounts above 30% generated negative profit of -102,494.13. This suggests that large discounts can damage margin.

Return risk is also visible. Furniture has the highest return volume and a return rate of 7.67%, compared with 3.65% for Office Supplies and 3.03% for Technology. Shipping risk is visible through Standard Class, which carries the largest sales volume but has the slowest average delivery time at 4.71 days.

## Visible Opportunities

The strongest opportunities are to grow Technology products, protect high-performing South-region playbooks, target Home Office customers, and use organic demand as a strength. Same Day shipping has the fastest delivery time at 0.40 days, which may be valuable for high-value or urgent customer orders.

There is also an opportunity to improve East performance by comparing product mix, campaign execution, and customer behavior against stronger regions.

## Recommended Business Actions

Leadership should prioritize Technology availability and campaigns, especially for Copiers, Accessories, Phones, and Machines. Discount approvals should be reviewed carefully above 30%, because the dashboard shows negative profit in that discount range.

The business should investigate Furniture returns, including product information, packaging, delivery handling, and quality issues. Regional leaders should study South performance and test transferable practices in East and West. Shipping teams should monitor Standard Class delivery performance and reserve faster shipping options for high-value orders.

## Limitations Of The Dashboard

The dashboard summarizes historical business data from 2024-01-01 through 2025-12-31. It shows patterns and relationships, but it does not prove that one factor directly causes another. For example, high discounts may be linked to low profit because discounts reduce margin, but discounts may also be used more often on products or regions that were already difficult to sell.

The dashboard also depends on the available fields. It does not include every possible driver, such as competitor actions, local events, exact inventory stockouts, customer acquisition cost, or detailed return reasons.

## Suggested Next Analysis

The next analysis should focus on discount profitability by category and sub-category, return reasons for Furniture, regional performance drivers in South versus East, and campaign return on investment by channel. A controlled test of discount levels would help leadership separate association from causation.
